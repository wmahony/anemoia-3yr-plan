Why the Revenue Ramp is Credible
Catalog compounds year over year

Every release made in Year 1 continues earning in Year 2 and Year 3. Streaming, publishing royalties, and physical back-catalog sales accumulate. Each year of the plan adds permanent earning assets — nothing resets.

Sync converts on a 12–18 month cycle

Sync relationships with agencies and music supervisors built in Year 1 begin converting to placements in Year 2. By Year 3, with an established pipeline across TV, film, and branded content, sync becomes the label's highest single revenue source.

Year 3 costs drop sharply

The $1M deal fund is fully deployed by end of Year 2. Year 3 operating costs are $535k — against $627k revenue. With 4 artists in full release cycle and no new advance capital required, the business reaches equilibrium.

Year 4 and beyond: Operating costs stabilize at ~$535k. Revenue continues to compound — streaming grows as the catalog deepens, sync placements recur annually, physical back-catalog sells continuously, and merchandise scales with the fanbase. Revenue Model
Source-by-source breakdown
Streaming figures assume $5,000 per million streams gross with a 50% label share, yielding $2,500 per million streams net to the label. This is a conservative industry-standard estimate. Sync and physical margins are based on mid-tier indie label benchmarks.
Revenue Sources — 3-Year Targets & Context
Sync Licensing
Y1 $15k Y2 $67k Y3 $207k
The label's highest-value revenue source by Year 3. Sync placements — in TV, film, advertising, and content libraries — pay a one-time licensing fee per use and can range from $5k for a small placement to $75k+ for a national campaign. Year 1 establishes relationships with boutique sync agencies and music supervisors. Year 2 sees those relationships convert to 2–3 placements. By Year 3, with an established pipeline and growing catalog, the label targets 6–8 placements annually. Anemoia's cinematic, textural aesthetic is well-suited to sync. Priority agencies to approach in Year 1: Musicbed, Marmoset, Secret Road, Artlist.
Streaming
Y1 $100k Y2 $130k Y3 $160k
Year 1 targets 50M streams across the roster at $2k/M net to the label — $100k. Year 2 grows to ~65M streams ($130k) and Year 3 to ~80M ($160k) on a conservative compounding basis, reflecting the same artist base with growing catalog depth rather than assuming new breakout growth. Every release added to the catalog continues earning indefinitely — Year 1 streams still contribute to Year 3 totals. Streaming and publishing together are the most predictable and compounding revenue sources in the model.
Merchandise
Y1 $9k Y2 $35k Y3 $80k
Standard apparel at approximately $22 margin (retail $35, cost of goods $13). Year 3's $80k represents roughly 3,600 units across 4 artists — achievable with seasonal drops and tour support. Margin improves significantly with limited-edition bundles: a hand-numbered vinyl, zine, and print set at $80–120 margin per unit. 200 bundle units on a single release generates $20k alone. Merchandise also serves as a brand-building vehicle — extending Anemoia's identity in physical form.
Physical — Vinyl & CD
Y1 $18k Y2 $35k Y3 $65k
Vinyl at $12 net margin per unit (retail ~$30, pressing + fulfillment ~$18). CD at $8 margin. Year 3's $65k represents approximately 3,000 vinyl and 1,500 CDs across the back-catalog and new releases. Initial pressings of 500–750 units per title; represses on strong performers are pure margin. Direct-to-consumer via Bandcamp and the label website maintains the highest margin. Select independent retail placement for visibility.
Publishing & Royalties
Y1 $50k Y2 $65k Y3 $80k
Performance royalties collected through ASCAP or BMI, plus mechanical royalties via the Mechanical Licensing Collective (MLC). These accumulate automatically on every stream, broadcast, and live performance — but only if properly registered. Publishing admin should be established in Year 1 Q1 via a publishing administrator (Songtrust or direct ASCAP/BMI registration). Year 3's $80k reflects three full years of catalog accrual across 4 artists. Often the most overlooked revenue line; entirely passive once set up correctly.
Events
Y1 $6k Y2 $13k Y3 $35k
Austin provides a strong foundation — a deep music scene, lower venue costs than New York or Los Angeles, and an audience receptive to independent labels. Quarterly label showcases at $5–7k net each, plus 1–2 out-of-market events by Year 3. Brand sponsorship of one event per year meaningfully improves per-event economics. Events also serve as catalog discovery moments — attendees stream more, buy physical, and share.

Investor Return Structure
01
Revenue Share — Catalog Royalty
Partner receives their revenue split on net receipts from release through the end of the JV term. Once the $2.2M investment is fully recouped from Anemoia's revenue share, the split improves from 70/30 to 80/20 — recognizing catalog performance and reducing the ongoing burden as the label scales. All recoupment calculations are transparent, auditable semi-annually, and based on agreed accounting definitions set at signing.

Best suited for: Major label JV partners and music-focused funds seeking catalog access with defined recoupment and a structured pathway to revenue participation.
Recommended
02
Equity — Convertible Note or SAFE
$1.70M raised at a $5–6M pre-money valuation cap. Converts on a future priced round or acquisition event. Return is exit-dependent — no current yield. Upside comes from label acquisition: distributors and major labels regularly acquire growing independent labels at 4–6× EBITDA multiples. At Year 4–5 revenue trajectory, that implies a $2.5–4M exit value.

Best suited for: Music-adjacent angel investors and funds comfortable with illiquid, long-horizon positions.
Alternative
03
Hybrid — Catalog-Backed Debt + Equity
$1M as a catalog-secured loan (the artist deal fund, secured against the master recordings), repaid from revenue beginning Year 3. $700k as equity at a lower valuation. The debt tranche is asset-backed and lower risk; the equity tranche captures acquisition upside. Allows the raise to be structured across two investor profiles simultaneously.

Best suited for: Sophisticated music investors or family offices with appetite for structured deals.

Deal Terms
Rights & Ownership
Master Ownership
Anemoia retains full ownership of all master recordings throughout and after the JV term.
License Grant
JV Partner receives an exclusive distribution license for the JV term only. All rights revert fully to Anemoia at expiry — no holdback periods.
Co-Ownership Option
Open to exploring on new recordings if it materially improves deal economics — reversion rights and buyout provisions required in either case.
Creative Control
A&R Authority
Anemoia retains sole and final A&R authority. JV Partner has no approval rights over signings or creative direction.
Marketing & Release
Collaborative — Anemoia holds final sign-off on all campaign strategy and release scheduling.
Right of First Refusal
Partner receives a 30-day ROFR on new Anemoia signings. If passed, Anemoia is free to place the artist elsewhere.
Exit & Reversion
Time-Based Reversion
All master licenses revert to Anemoia at end of term. No holdback periods.
Performance Triggers
If partner fails minimum marketing spend or streaming benchmarks, Anemoia may elect early reversion of affected recordings.
Buyout Clause
Anemoia retains buyout rights on any co-owned recordings at a pre-agreed valuation formula at any point during or after the term.
Sync Licensing
Sync approvals delegated to Anemoia for operational efficiency — 1-stop clearance on all JV recordings. A designated Anemoia sync rep holds approval authority to eliminate clearance delays. Partner's sync team actively pitches JV catalog through their placement network.
Infrastructure Access
Partner provides committed distribution infrastructure plus collaborative access to their sync licensing network, radio promotion team, and international office network. This access is a core component of the JV's value proposition — not incidental.
